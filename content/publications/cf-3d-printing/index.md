abstract: >
  Continuous carbon fiber (CCF) 3D printing on multi-axis FFF systems can
  dramatically improve structural performance, but existing slicers ignore
  load-path alignment and generate suboptimal fiber trajectories.
  We present a G-code planning pipeline that integrates finite element
  analysis (FEA), SIMP topology optimization, and surface geodesic path
  planning to align fiber deposition along principal stress directions.
  Our XYA-axis motion planner generates helical winding and stress-aligned
  infill layers with a 16× G-code generation speedup over naive approaches.
  Parts printed with our planner exhibit significantly higher specific
  stiffness compared to standard rectilinear CCF toolpaths.
