abstract: >
  Deploying VLA policies in real-world robotic systems demands inference
  latency and throughput far beyond what standard serving stacks provide.
  We systematically characterize the inference bottlenecks of flow-matching
  VLA architectures and propose a suite of optimizations including action-chunk
  caching, speculative denoising, and heterogeneous GPU-CPU pipeline scheduling.
  Our infrastructure enables sub-20ms end-to-end policy inference on commodity
  hardware without accuracy degradation, bridging the gap between research
  models and production robot deployments.
